# Investor Q&A

## Q1: Is this just another smart speaker?

No. Smart speakers are mostly consumer assistants inside closed ecosystems. ESPAndyAI EDGE is a business-oriented edge node connected to AndyAI Director, with local logs, permissions, workflows, and human approval.

## Q2: Does the ESP32 run the whole AI model locally?

No. The realistic first version uses ESP32 as the physical node and AndyAI Director as the brain. The node handles audio capture, local events, basic device control, status, and logging. Director handles reasoning, memory, policy, and tool execution.

## Q3: Why not just use a phone?

A phone is personal and screen-oriented. ESPAndyAI EDGE is spatial, persistent, dedicated, and can be placed in an office, workshop, classroom, counter, or machine area.

## Q4: What is the moat?

The moat is not the ESP32 board alone. The moat is the combination of Director governance, memory, device registry, voice workflows, local logging, approval gates, and reusable business deployment templates.

## Q5: What is the first customer?

Developers and friendly pilot customers. The first serious B2B customers are documentation-heavy teams, workshops, AI automation agencies, and small businesses needing voice workflows.

## Q6: What are the biggest risks?

Hardware support, API costs, certification, unclear positioning, and security. The plan mitigates these through a staged rollout: PoC, 5 prototypes, 50 dev kits, pilots, then commercial batch.

## Q7: How does this make money?

Through hardware sales, SaaS subscriptions, pilot deployments, custom installations, support, and licensing.

## Q8: Why now?

Edge AI, voice agents, AIoT, and affordable ESP32-class hardware make physical AI nodes practical. The market is already moving toward edge intelligence and AI-connected devices.

## Q9: What funding is needed?

A mini pre-seed of $50,000–$150,000 can fund PoC, demo, early kits, and pilots. A stronger pre-seed of $250,000–$500,000 can fund a small team, 100–300 devices, more mature software, and serious pilot deployments.

## Q10: What is the 12-month target?

A working product line with 100–300 devices, several paid pilots, recurring Director revenue, and investor-ready evidence for a larger commercial series.
